Problem Gap Analysis
Design Focus Representative Studies Concise Gap
All-in-one wearable assistance Yu et al., 2024 (VisiGlasses)
Mathis et al., 2025 (LifeInsight)		 No unified, real-time navigation + interaction + privacy in one device.
Fine-grained object grasp guidance Yu et al., 2024 (VisiGlasses) FOV and occlusion still break precise hand-level grasp guidance.
Privacy-preserving assistive vision Gurari et al., 2019 (VizWiz-Priv) Few systems blur sensitive content automatically during everyday live use.
Low-latency & connectivity-independent Sridhar et al., 2025 (NaviSense) Cloud dependence causes latency and privacy risks; reliable on-device needed.
Persistent world model for decisions (Cross-cutting limitation across systems) Most solutions lack a fused world model with 3D coordinates.
Research Questions (RQs)
  • RQ1: Navigation How does ARGUS change blind/low-vision users’ indoor navigation safety and speed compared to their usual method?
  • RQ2: Object Finding How well does ARGUS help users find and pick up a specific object, and which guidance style do they prefer (left/right, clock directions, distance cues)?
  • RQ3: Privacy How do ARGUS privacy features affect users’ trust and comfort in public, and what information do they want the system to avoid describing?
